A Race Against Waste

With the e-waste crisis accelerating, immediate action is needed through scalable technologies and circular strategies that can transform risk into resilience.

Urgency and Innovation in E-Waste Management

The Cost of Inaction

Improper e-waste disposal has serious consequences. Only 22.3% of global e-waste is formally recycled, while the remainder often ends up in landfills or is illegally dumped. Toxic substances like lead, mercury, and brominated plastics leach into the environment, contaminating soil and water, and posing significant health risks to workers and communities.

The Case for Decentralized Recovery

One of the most promising solutions lies in decentralized, small-scale technologies that allow local processing of waste. These modular systems can operate in communities without the need for large, centralized infrastructure. MICROfactories™ exemplify this approach, recovering materials from just a few kilograms of electronics and making recycling more accessible and cost-effective.

Designing for the End from the Start

Eco-design is essential in mitigating future e-waste. By engineering products to be easier to disassemble and recycle, manufacturers can drastically improve material recovery rates. Meanwhile, smart collection systems powered by the Internet of Things (IoT) help monitor, trace, and optimize the collection and separation of e-waste, increasing user engagement and system efficiency.

Closing the Loop

When combined, these strategies create a powerful closed-loop model. Materials are reused instead of discarded, pollution is prevented before it starts, and resource cycles are shortened. Through smart design, localized recovery, and connected systems, we can transition from a linear consumption model to a circular, sustainable electronics ecosystem.
