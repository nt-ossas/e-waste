Creating Value from Waste

A Growing Mountain of Value

The world now produces over 62 million tonnes of e-waste each year, an amount that continues to grow alongside technological advancement and consumer demand. Beneath the surface of this waste stream lies an untapped wealth of resources: gold, lithium, cobalt, copper, and rare earth elements—critical for producing smartphones, electric vehicles, and clean energy technologies. These materials are not only scarce but vital to sustaining modern industries.

From Trash to Treasure

Traditionally discarded electronics are now viewed as “urban mines,” rich in valuable materials. Unlike conventional mining, e-waste recovery leverages existing resources embedded in used products, significantly reducing the environmental footprint. Advanced methods such as hydrometallurgy and pyrometallurgy allow for efficient recovery, while microrecycling opens up new possibilities for localized processing, minimizing logistics and emissions.

Innovative Processes for a Circular Future

Emerging technologies like biometallurgy and modular MICROfactories™ are revolutionizing recovery techniques, enabling even small volumes of e-waste to yield substantial returns. These processes not only reduce dependence on mining but also contribute to industrial resilience and environmental protection. They transform materials once considered refuse into crucial inputs for new innovation cycles.

Circular Value Chains and Green Jobs

E-waste recycling doesn’t just reduce landfill waste, it generates economic value and creates local employment. As circular supply chains take hold, manufacturing becomes more sustainable and resilient. What was once a disposal challenge becomes a new industrial opportunity, where waste is continuously reintegrated into the economy, supporting a greener and more resource-efficient future.
