Creating Value from Waste

Urban Mining

Urban mining is revolutionizing how we view electronic waste. Rather than seeing discarded devices as garbage, we recognize them as valuable deposits of precious metals and rare earth elements.

A single ton of smartphones contains more gold than a ton of gold ore, making e-waste recycling not just environmentally responsible but economically attractive.

Circular Economy Models

The circular economy approach aims to keep products and materials in use for as long as possible, extracting their maximum value before recovering and regenerating products and materials at the end of their service life.

By implementing circular principles in electronics design and recycling, we can reduce waste while creating new business opportunities and jobs in refurbishment, repair, and materials recovery.

Job Creation

The e-waste recycling sector creates up to 30 times more jobs than traditional landfill operations for the same amount of material. These include positions in collection, disassembly, materials processing, and manufacturing.

As the industry grows more sophisticated, it also creates opportunities for highly skilled technical and engineering roles.

Resource Security

By recovering critical raw materials from e-waste, countries can reduce dependence on imported resources and mitigate supply risks for electronics manufacturing.

This "urban mining" approach becomes increasingly important as natural deposits of rare earth elements and precious metals become more difficult and expensive to extract.
