E-Waste solutions

Focus on Transformation and Potential

The world generates over 62 million tons of e-waste yearly, rich in valuable materials. Recognizing it as an urban mine can create economic and sustainable opportunities.

Highlighting the Urgency and the Solution

Only 22.3% of e-waste is recycled, leading to economic loss and pollution. Modular technologies and eco-design can enhance local recycling and reduce waste.

Science and Chemicals Parts

Chemistry is key for metal recovery from e-waste. Techniques like hydrometallurgy and biometallurgy offer sustainable ways to extract metals without toxins.

Robotics for Automated Disassembly

Robotics simplifies e-waste disassembly, enhancing worker safety. Automated systems can accurately separate components, improving recycling efficiency.

Artificial Intelligence in Material Recovery

AI optimizes recycling systems, enhancing material separation and process efficiency. Combining AI with blockchain ensures smart, traceable e-waste management.
